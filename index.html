Welcome to Project DataREACH!

Big data can soon reach every corner of the globe to improve quality of life and make a positive impact on the medical field.

Mission Statement

As data science continues to grow as an integral part of life in the 21 st century, its applications around the world continue to diversify and change the status quo. Our mission is to harness the powerful applications of machine learning and predictive analytics to make a positive impact on medicine in developing nations. Through the training of medical professionals on the fundamentals of data science as well as assisting in the creation of advanced analytics pipelines via machine learning, we hope to make groundbreaking strides in integrating AI in medicine in developing nations.

Guyana 2015


Started in August 2015 with founder Vikash Singh taking a three week trip to Guyana fostering connections with and giving presentations to hospitals (including Georgetown Public Hospital Corp, Balwant Singh Hospital, and Hanoman Medical Center), universities, and government members (received commendation from Guyana Ministry of Health/NCI) about how predictive analytics can start to be implemented/the advantages of predictive analysis/machine learning when applied to medicine, especially disease diagnosis via clinical data sets. For this initiative, he was commended by the nation’s Ministry of Health and National Cancer Institute as they saw the potential that such a program could have.

Cameroon 2016


In the summer of 2016 Project DataREACH expanded with a project in Cameroon thanks to a collaboration with Songhai Labs, which is global Knowledge Broker Platform for the African Continent with the ambition to positively transform African societies by sharing knowledge and innovations across countries and across sectors. We are currently working on a proposal to obtain some data from medical entities in Cameroon to begin looking into ways in which machine learning pipelines can usefully be created. We are also planning remote basic training sessions to enable motivated medical professionals in Cameroon to apply data science techniques to data they have access to and begin discussion of how these skills will impact the nation long term.
